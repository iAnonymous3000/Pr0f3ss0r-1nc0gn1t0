A Faraday bag or pouch is useful when you have a specific need to temporarily isolate a phone, key fob, hardware wallet, or other small wireless device from cellular, Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, NFC, or similar radio signals. It is a focused signal-control tool, not a universal privacy requirement and not a substitute for reducing the data stored on the device.

What to look for:

- A size that fully closes around the device without strain
- Independent shielding or attenuation information from the maker
- A closure design that is easy to seal correctly every time
- A quick self-test routine before travel, such as checking whether calls, Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, and location updates stop while the device is sealed
- A clear reason to carry it, since it also blocks legitimate calls, tracking recovery, and emergency connectivity while sealed
