Honda is no longer a card in the Vehicles category because the Civic/Accord recommendation is too broad to be a solid pick. Honda equipment varies heavily by model, year, trim, market, and infotainment package. On many older/base configurations, HondaLink is phone-linked over Bluetooth instead of being backed by an embedded cellular modem, but that makes base LX/EX-style trims candidates, not recommendations.

Avoid Touring, Elite, hybrid, and embedded-TCU trims unless you are deliberately accepting the telematics hardware. Honda's own [2018-2020 Accord/Accord Hybrid OTA material](https://static.nhtsa.gov/odi/tsbs/2021/MC-10204245-0001.pdf) describes a built-in telematics control unit used for HondaLink, Wi-Fi hotspot, emergency calling, stolen-vehicle locator, driver feedback, and other connected services. Use Honda's [HondaLink compatibility checker](https://mygarage.honda.com/s/hondalink-product-compatibility), run the VIN, inspect the car, and treat Honda as a case-by-case research path.

The mechanical caveat is separate from privacy: Honda's documented [1.5L turbo oil-dilution warranty extension](https://static.nhtsa.gov/odi/tsbs/2019/MC-10158743-0001.pdf) covered certain 2016-2018 Civic and 2017-2018 CR-V vehicles. Similar problems in the 2018-2022 Accord have been alleged in class-action filings but are not part of that documented extension. Prefer naturally aspirated engines when you want to avoid that risk path.
