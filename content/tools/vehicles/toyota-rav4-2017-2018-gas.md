The 2017-2018 gas RAV4 is the Toyota crossover pick because it combines the pre-2020 telematics window with standard Toyota Safety Sense P. Toyota says [2017 RAV4](https://pressroom.toyota.com/2017-rav4-gains-new-grades-standard-toyota-safety-sense/) and [2018 RAV4](https://pressroom.toyota.com/2018-toyota-rav4-ready-more-action-new-adventure-le-hybrid-grade/) models had TSS-P standard on all grades, including pre-collision with pedestrian detection, lane departure alert with steering assist, automatic high beams, and dynamic radar cruise control.

The 2014-2016 gas RAV4 may still be clean, but it lacks this safety/privacy sweet spot, so it is no longer the featured card. Toyota's 3G retirement chart names the 2012-2014 RAV4 EV, not the 2014-2018 gas RAV4, while Toyota's [2020 Connected Services table](https://www.toyota.com/content/dam/toyota/audio-multimedia/pdf/new/MY20_Connected_Services_Table.pdf) lists 2020 RAV4 connected-service availability by audio package. This rule covers gas models only. The RAV4 EV is excluded.
