iCloud is only worth recommending from a privacy perspective if you explicitly turn on Advanced Data Protection. Key points include:
- Advanced Data Protection raises many major iCloud categories to end-to-end encryption
- iCloud Backup, iCloud Drive, Photos, and Notes benefit substantially once Advanced Data Protection is enabled
- Mail, Contacts, and Calendar are still not end-to-end encrypted even with Advanced Data Protection enabled
- Some collaboration and sharing modes still fall back to weaker protection
- Reasonable choice for people deep in the Apple ecosystem, but the default setup is not enough
